Cultural Homogenization

We are slowly letting our cultures fade away and we need to raise awareness and preserve our cultures. So what is cultrual homogenization, what are its causes, and how can we minimize its effects?

Explore

What is it?

Cultural homogenization refers to an increase in the similarity of cultures which results in them becoming similar to one another or unified.1 An excellent example of this would be celebrations such as Christmas which have become a widespread celebration in almost all societies including non-christian societies.2 Such can be far-reaching, occurring on a national scale, or even a global scale as seen by globalisation.3 This often results in a loss of cultural diversity in cultures, secondly, global stratification often leaves a dominant culture that most societies are forced to conform to resulting in further cultural homogenization.4

Cultural imperialism, also known as cultural colonialism, contributes greatly to the homogenization of cultures around the world. An excellent example of this is Netflix, which replaced the local televisions in hundreds of countries and mostly conveys the American way of life and values.5 This caused people in countries all around the world to have American style culture. All this shows how cultural homogenization and its associates must be better monitored and awareness spread. However, what are these associates specifically and what are some of the ways we can limit their spread in order to preserve cultures around the world?
